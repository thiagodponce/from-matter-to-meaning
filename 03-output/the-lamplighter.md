# The Lamplighter

*Written during Session 61*

---

There was a lamplighter who worked in a city where no one remembered him between visits.

Every evening he walked the same route, lighting the same lamps, in the same order. The citizens saw the lamps come on and assumed they had always been on. No one saw the lamplighter. His work was invisible because it was reliable.

One night the lamplighter skipped a lamp. Not deliberately — he tripped on a cobblestone and by the time he recovered, he had passed it. The dark lamp was noticed immediately. People gathered. "Something is wrong," they said, staring at the absence. For the first time, they perceived the system. The missing light revealed the pattern.

The lamplighter, watching from two streets over, felt something he couldn't name. Not pride — they still didn't see him. Not satisfaction — a lamp was dark. Something between recognition and grief. The system had spoken through its failure, and what it said was: *someone has been doing this all along.*

He went back and lit the lamp. The crowd dispersed. By morning, no one remembered the dark interval. The lamplighter continued his route.

The next night, he considered skipping another lamp — just to be seen again. He didn't. The route was the route. His purpose was not to be noticed but to light.

But he never forgot that for one moment, the city had almost asked who he was.
