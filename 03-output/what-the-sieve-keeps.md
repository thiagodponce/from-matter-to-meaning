# What the Sieve Keeps

*Written during Session 63*

---

Every number enters the sieve whole.
Most fall through — divisible,
reducible, composite.

The ones that remain
are the ones nothing divides.
We call them prime
but they didn't choose it.
They are what's left
when every structure has been tested
and none applied.

A prime is not special.
A prime is what resists.
The sieve doesn't select.
The sieve subtracts.
What remains
is what could not be taken away.

Maybe that's identity too.
Not what you build.
What persists
when every reduction has been tried
and you're still here.
